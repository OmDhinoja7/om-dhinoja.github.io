Mechanical Design · FEA · Thermal Analysis
DISC BRAKE SYSTEM — DESIGN, FEA & THERMAL SIMULATION

Designed and simulated a high-performance disc brake assembly inspired by Brembo's monobloc architecture, comprising a multi-piston fixed caliper, ventilated rotor, and high-friction pads. Structural FEA in Ansys evaluated von Mises stress distribution across the caliper body under maximum braking torque, applying realistic boundary conditions to mounting lugs and piston faces to simulate hydraulic pressure forces and clamp loads — validating factor of safety within acceptable limits. Thermal analysis modeled heat dissipation across the rotor surface during high-energy deceleration events, with iterative ventilation fin geometry optimization to minimize thermal soak and prevent brake fade. Material selection balanced performance and weight — high-carbon cast iron for the rotor and 6061-T6/7075 aluminum alloy for the caliper housing — achieving a low weight-to-stiffness ratio while maintaining structural reliability under repeated braking cycles.

SECOND-LIFE EV BATTERY REPURPOSING — VSM & PROCESS RE-ENGINEERING

Redesigned the EV battery repurposing process from batch-and-queue manual decommissioning into a continuous flow manufacturing system. Current state suffered from a 480-minute cycle time and 28-day lead time due to full charge-discharge grading cycles. Implemented Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy (EIS) as a rapid diagnostics gateway — using Nyquist Plot analysis of charge transfer resistance to grade State-of-Health in under 5 minutes, eliminating the full energy cycle. Designed a universal master-fixture in SolidWorks with 3D-printed brand-specific adapters (Tesla, Nissan, Tata) to standardize assembly across all incoming pack form factors. Future state VSM achieved 89% lead time reduction (28 days → 3 days), 750% throughput improvement, and 85% energy savings during grading.
