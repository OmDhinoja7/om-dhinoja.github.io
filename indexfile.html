DISC BRAKE SYSTEM — DESIGN, FEA & THERMAL SIMULATION
Designed and simulated a high-performance disc brake assembly inspired by
Brembo's monobloc architecture, comprising a multi-piston ﬁxed caliper,
ventilated rotor, and high-friction pads. Structural FEA in Ansys evaluated von
Mises stress distribution across the caliper body under maximum braking torque,
applying realistic boundary conditions to mounting lugs and piston faces to
simulate hydraulic pressure forces and clamp loads — validating factor of safety

rotor surface during high-energy deceleration events, with iterative ventilation ﬁn
geometry optimization to minimize thermal soak and prevent brake fade. Material
selection balanced performance and weight — high-carbon cast iron for the rotor
and 6061-T6/7075 aluminum alloy for the caliper housing — achieving a low
weight-to-stiffness ratio while maintaining structural reliability under repeated
braking cycles.
